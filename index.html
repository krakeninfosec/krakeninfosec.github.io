Common Questions

Frequently Asked

Do I need a degree to get into cybersecurity? +
No. The cybersecurity field is one of the most skills-based industries. Most practicing professionals are self-taught or hold certifications rather than degrees. What matters is demonstrating practical skills through labs, certifications like OSCP, and a strong portfolio.
What certification should I start with? +
For offensive security, start with eJPT then work toward OSCP. For defensive security, CompTIA Security+ is a solid foundation. The most important thing is to pair any certification with hands-on practice — TryHackMe, HackTheBox, and home labs.
Is the wiki really free forever? +
Yes. The cybersecurity career wiki, roadmaps, and educational content on this site will always be free. Knowledge should not be gated. Premium products like handbooks and courses are separate paid offerings that help fund the free content.
How do I get started in penetration testing? +
Start with networking and Linux fundamentals. Then move to TryHackMe beginner paths. Get your eJPT certification. Build a home lab. Complete 20+ HackTheBox machines. Then pursue OSCP. Document everything in a GitHub portfolio and blog.
Do you offer consulting for small businesses? +
Yes. We work with businesses of all sizes — from startups to enterprises. Get in touch to discuss your specific needs and we will provide a proposal tailored to your budget and requirements.
How often is the wiki updated? +
The wiki is updated regularly — new role pages, concept pages, and tool overviews are added consistently. The goal is to eventually cover all 126 mapped roles in complete detail. Updates are announced on the blog.
